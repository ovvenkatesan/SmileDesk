## Design Paradigm: The "Consumer Health / Wellness" Look

We are basing our visual foundation on the aesthetics of modern Consumer Health platforms (like Calm, Ro/Roman, and Hims/Hers). 

### Why We Like It:
These platforms excel at taking clinical, potentially stressful topics and making them feel approachable, premium, and calm. This perfectly aligns with the "Neighborhood Nurse" persona of the Voice Agent.

### What We Are Emulating:
- Soft, non-aggressive color palettes (sage greens, muted blues, warm off-whites).
- Ample whitespace to prevent cognitive overload.
- Rounded shape language for UI components (buttons, cards).
- Applying this "consumer-grade" aesthetic even to the B2B Owner Dashboard, avoiding the "dense enterprise spreadsheet" look.

## Design Principles (Synthesized)

### Visual Style
**DO:**
- Use a soft, calming color palette that feels hygienic but warm.
- Rely heavily on whitespace to create a sense of 'breathing room' and unhurried calm.
- Use rounded corners on cards and buttons to convey approachability.

**DON'T:**
- Use stark contrasts (like pure black and white) or harsh, 'hacker-style' dark modes.
- Use intimidating clinical colors (like aggressive alert reds) unless absolutely necessary for errors.

### Content Hierarchy (Widget)
**DO:**
- Present "Next 3 Steps" instantly. Anticipate the user's need based on the page they are viewing (e.g., "I'm in pain", "Check insurance").
- Keep the widget interface primarily focused on action buttons rather than a massive wall of chat text.

**DON'T:**
- Present a blank "How can I help you?" chat box that forces the user to do the cognitive work of typing out their problem.

### Content Hierarchy (Owner Dashboard)
**DO:**
- Surface the "After-Hours ROI Snapshot" (dollar amounts recovered, appointments booked) as the massive, central metric upon login.
- Treat the clinic owner like a consumer: make the data beautiful and immediately rewarding.

**DON'T:**
- Bury the ROI under technical metrics like "Total API calls" or "Transcription accuracy rate."

## 1. Opening & Recommendation
**Saga:** Based on the "Consumer Health" references (Ro, Calm) and the "Neighborhood Nurse" persona, we need to translate those vibes into specific UI terminologies so the developers and designers know exactly what to build.

Here is what I recommend for the core Design Style:

**1. UI Visual Style: "Minimal Soft" (A variant of Flat Design)**
- **Rationale:** We avoid heavy shadows, complex gradients, or "glassmorphism" (which can feel too techy). Instead, we use very flat, clean surfaces with significant whitespace and large, rounded border radii. It feels hygienic but comfortable.

**2. Design Aesthetic: "Modern Clinical / Consumer Wellness"**
- **Rationale:** It bridges the gap between medical trust and consumer ease. It relies on clean lines, lack of clutter, and highly legible data visualization (for the dashboard).

**3. Typography Direction:**
- **Headlines:** A clean, friendly Geometric Sans-Serif (like *Poppins* or *Outfit*). It feels modern and approachable.
- **Body/UI Text:** A highly legible Neo-grotesque Sans-Serif (like *Inter* or *Inter*). Essential for reading medical data or dashboard metrics without eye strain.

**Saga:** We are almost done with the Brief! The last piece of the puzzle is establishing the **Tone of Voice**. 

To be clear, this isn't about the marketing copy on the website; this is specifically about the *personality* of the Voice AI Agent itself—how it speaks, how it handles errors (like when it misunderstands someone), and the microcopy on the Business Dashboard.

Based on everything we've discussed—targeting anxious local patients and logistical-minded foreign tourists, aiming for a "native, empathetic concierge" feel, and wanting to build trust instantly—I suggest the following Tone Attributes:

**Tone Attributes:**
1. **Empathetic & Validating:** It doesn't just answer questions; it acknowledges feelings. (Essential for patients in pain or experiencing dental anxiety).
2. **Clear & Grounded:** It avoids overly complex medical jargon unless specifically asked, keeping instructions simple and actionable (vital for non-native speakers or stressed callers).
3. **Calm & Unhurried:** It uses "Prosodic Alignment" to never sound rushed, projecting the patient, unbothered competence of a high-end concierge.
4. **Professional yet Warm:** It feels like a highly trained medical assistant, not a quirky startup bot.
